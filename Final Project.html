The Sinister Source of Our Conservation Funding

Final Project: Story Telling With Data

Peter W. Schoffelen

Intro

Despite declining numbers in hunters, the excise tax appropriations from Robert-Pittman rose substantially during the Obama administration(most likely explained by gun sales driven by fear of stricter gun laws), and that the decline (less fearful of stricter gun laws) during the Trump administration makes aspect like a Backpack Tax (a tax on equipment sold from REI) possibly good solutions to sustaining conservation funding while also advancing more progressive gun control.

Project Structure

Basics

    Aim: To have readers recognize that the funding of conservation is more complex than one might intially imagine and that if we care about conservation we must ensure its funding by diversfying the sources of tax revenue earmaked for conservation programs
    Measures of Success: call to action conversion and social media shares I think would be good metrics for the success of the this story; A full suite of more in-depth analtyics, such as dwell time, would be a useful complement to the primary measures.

Outline/Wireframe

Hook: Did you know that a Tax on hunting equipment accounts for an average of $713 million dollars in appropriations towards conservation from 2012 to 2016 and was the single largest source of federal funding for convservation? The next larget source of federal fundings for conservation is less that half that amount at just $338 million per year average from 2012 to 2016 with the revenue coming from general tax revenue paid for by we the people.

Building: The funding from taxing hunters is known as the North American Model of Wildlife Conservation and is widly considered one of the most succesful convervation programs in the world, briniging many spcecies back from the brink of extiction.

Luring:The money also is the primary source for much of the maitenance and managment of resources enjoyed by a large array outdoor recreation entusiats such as Birdwatchers, hikers, backpackers, and mountain bikers. But this funding appears to be at risk as the numbers hunters in the united states has been on a slow and steady decline since the the 1920s and there is no current plan to broaden the tax base beyond hunters.
